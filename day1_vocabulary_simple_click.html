Day 1 – Vocabulary

Tap the word or 🔊 to hear it. No settings needed.

Word Meaning Example Sentence
Versatile /ˈvɜːr-sə-tl/ Able to adapt to many different functions or activities. "This tool is really versatile; you can use it for cooking, crafting, and even repairs."
Meticulous /mə-ˈtɪk-jə-ləs/ Showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise. "He is meticulous about keeping his desk organized, arranging everything perfectly."
Ingenious /ɪn-ˈdʒiː-ni-əs/ Very clever or inventive. "She came up with an ingenious solution to the problem that no one else had thought of."
Resilient /rɪ-ˈzɪl-jənt/ Able to recover quickly from difficulties; tough and able to bounce back. "Even after setbacks, he's very resilient and keeps moving forward."
Astute /ə-ˈstuːt/ Perceptive; able to understand situations or people clearly and quickly. "He made an astute observation that changed the course of the discussion."
Perceptive /pər-ˈsɛp-tɪv/ Having a keen ability to notice and understand things that are not obvious; observant. "She's very perceptive and always picks up on subtle changes in people's moods."
